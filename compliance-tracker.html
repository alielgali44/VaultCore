How it works

Three steps. Your team does two of them.

The system handles everything in between — automatically, every day.

1

Log a compliance record

Staff fill a simple web form — project name, donor, document type, and expiry date. Takes 60 seconds. No spreadsheet, no email thread.

2

System watches automatically

Vault Core scans every record daily. It sends the right alert to the right person at 30, 15, and 3 days before expiry — with no human involved.

3

Upload proof and close

After submitting the document, staff upload the PDF proof. A manager approves it. The record is closed and the full history is saved.

Alert schedule

Four-tier escalation system

Each tier fires automatically. No one has to remember to check anything.

30-day warning

Early notice sent to the responsible staff member and team lead.

15-day warning

Reminder with urgency — time to act if the document isn't filed yet.

3-day warning

Final alert. If proof isn't uploaded after this, escalation begins.

ED escalation

Deadline passed with no proof? The Executive Director is alerted directly — automatically.

What's included

Built for how NGOs actually operate.

Every feature in the system was designed around a real compliance failure mode.

Proof-of-submission vault

Staff upload the stamped PDF directly — a URA acknowledgment, signed MOU, or NSSF receipt. Stored locally, searchable instantly. When an auditor asks, you retrieve it in seconds, not days.

100% local — no cloud exposure

Every record, every document, every alert log is stored on your own server. No SaaS vendor. No third-party cloud. Fully donor-compliant and safe for sensitive grant data.

Executive compliance dashboard

The ED and management see a live Red/Amber/Green health view of every donor obligation — filterable by status, urgency, and donor. No login, no training, opens in any browser.

Full audit trail

Every status change is permanently logged — who logged it, who approved it, when, and why. When a donor or auditor asks for evidence, you have a clean timestamped record, not a WhatsApp search.
