How it works

Three inputs. One submission-ready report.

No templates, no manual reconciliation. Upload your data and the system handles the rest.

1

Upload your budget

Drop your Excel budget file and enter your donor name, grant reference, and reporting period. The system reads your actual structure — no template required.

2

System verifies

Every line item is read, categorised, and reconciled. Budget vs. actual figures are compared automatically, variances are calculated, and risk flags are raised where needed.

3

Report delivered

You receive an editable DOCX with verified figures, a variance section, flagged items with explanations, and a complete narrative draft — ready for your finance manager to review and submit.

What's in the report

Six sections. Nothing missing.

The output follows the structure donors expect — so your finance team reviews rather than writes from scratch.

01

Executive Summary

A plain-language overview of the reporting period — total expenditure, budget utilisation rate, and any headline variances.

02

Budget vs. Actual Overview

Side-by-side comparison of approved budget against actual expenditure, across every budget line — formatted for direct use in your submission.

03

Expenditure by Category

Spending broken down by category (personnel, activities, overhead, etc.) with percentage of total spend and percentage of budget consumed.

04

Variance Analysis

Every line that deviated from budget by more than 10% is flagged, with the deviation amount, percentage, and a draft explanation for the narrative.

05

Financial Risk Flags

Lines with over-expenditure, zero spend, or unusual patterns are surfaced with context — so your finance manager can address them before submission.

06

Donor Narrative Text

A full draft narrative written around your figures — covering utilisation, variances, and forward outlook. Editable in the DOCX before you sign off.
